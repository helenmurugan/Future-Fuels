Why Future Fuels?


If you are interested in learning about the future of the transportation industry, you have come to the right place! Future Fuels keeps up-to-speed with the latest scientific research in the transportation industry and provides an informative and unbiased overview of possible alternative fuels, for the future of road, air and water transport.


What's Wrong With Petrol and Diesel?


Combustion of fossil fuels such as petrol and diesel produce CO2 and other emissions into the atmosphere which trap heat within the Earthâ€™s atmosphere and contribute towards global warming. In response to European targets to reduce CO2 emissions, a law has been introduced banning the sale of new petrol and diesel cars from 2035. We will need alternative fuels or methods to power our cars, which do not produce harmful emissions. This ban focusses on cars, but in future other modes of transport such as planes and ships will need to start reducing their CO2 emissions as well.


What does a good Future Fuel look like?


There is no single answer to this question! A good fuel depends entirely on the end user requirements. For example, a business person travelling a short distance to work every day may be happy with a battery car that can be recharged overnight, however, drivers of battery-powered trucks who may be travelling cross country daily cannot wait hours to recharge their batteries in the middle of long journeys.
