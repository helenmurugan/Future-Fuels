Batteries

In Europe the sale of new electric and hybrid vehicles is expected to reach 30% by 2025

Electric vehicles use a lithium battery to store energy, when charged directly from renewable sources such as solar and wind this process is considered carbon-neutral. As no chemical fuels are used to power the vehicles, no emissions are produced at the exhaust.

Batteries are well-suited to smaller modes of transport such as motorbikes and cars, but can also be sensible for fleets of trucks depending on their intended use. There are already a wide range of battery vehicle providers and electrical recharging points across Europe, making it easier to transition to electric fleets.

  • Ideal solution for motorbikes and small passenger vehicles.
  • Can be recharged on site.
  • Low cost to run.
  • Carbon-neutral when charged from renewables.
  • No gaseous emissions at the tailpipe.
  • Long recharge times (hours).
  • Unsuitable for long distance travel.
  • Large vehicles require very heavy batteries.
  • Low energy efficiency for large vehicles.
  • Heavy batteries means reduced capacity onboard.
  • High upfront costs
